# Observational Constraints

This document maps the Null Ring Chronology Bubble (NRCB) model directly against modern experimental and observational data. Each constraint represents a requirement the model must satisfy in order to remain viable.

---

## Design Philosophy

The NRCB model is not designed to explain new observations.  
It is designed to **avoid contradiction with existing ones**.

All exotic behavior is confined to regions that current instruments cannot resolve.

---

## Gravitational Wave Constraints (LIGO / Virgo / KAGRA)

### Observations
- Binary black hole inspiral waveforms match General Relativity.
- Merger and ringdown phases are consistent with Kerr quasi-normal modes.
- No statistically significant deviations detected across many events.

### NRCB Requirements
- The exterior spacetime must match Kerr geometry during inspiral.
- Effective compactness must be indistinguishable from a black hole.
- Ringdown frequencies must be Kerr-like.
- Any deviations must occur below the region probed by quasi-normal modes.

### Compliance Strategy
The NRCB enforces exact Kerr behavior in Regions A and B.  
The chronology bubble exists only in Region D, below the effective observational depth of gravitational-wave probes.

---

## Black Hole Imaging Constraints (Event Horizon Telescope)

### Observations
- The black hole shadows of M87* and Sgr A* match Kerr predictions.
- Photon ring size and distortion agree with rotating GR models.
- Polarization structure indicates Kerr-like spacetime geometry.

### NRCB Requirements
- The photon orbit radius must match Kerr.
- Light ray trajectories must remain Kerr-like where imaged.
- No macroscopic deviations near the photon sphere.

### Compliance Strategy
The NRCB preserves Kerr geometry at and outside the photon orbit.  
Exotic causal structure occurs at smaller radii than those affecting the shadow.

---

## Stellar Orbit Constraints (Galactic Center)

### Observations
- Stellar orbits around Sgr A* match GR predictions.
- Periapsis precession consistent with Kerr spacetime.
- No unexplained deviations observed.

### NRCB Requirements
- Orbital dynamics outside the compact object must be Kerr-like.
- No anomalous precession or instability allowed.

### Compliance Strategy
Regions A and B are defined to be observationally identical to Kerr spacetime, ensuring all stellar orbit data remain valid.

---

## Gravitational Lensing Constraints

### Observations
- Strong lensing produces Einstein rings consistent with GR.
- Time-delay lensing matches standard potentials.
- Microlensing surveys show no exotic caustic structures.

### NRCB Requirements
- Lensing signatures must match Kerr black holes.
- No multiple throat or wormhole-like lensing patterns.
- No anomalous light-curve features.

### Compliance Strategy
All lensing probes Regions A and B only.  
The chronology bubble is localized at scales far smaller than lensing resolution.

---

## Weak-Field Gravity Constraints

### Observations
- Solar system tests confirm GR to high precision.
- Frame-dragging measured by satellites.
- GPS timing relies on GR corrections.

### NRCB Requirements
- Weak-field behavior must reduce exactly to GR.
- No new long-range forces or corrections allowed.

### Compliance Strategy
The NRCB introduces no modification to GR in the weak-field regime.

---

## Electromagnetic Accretion Constraints

### Observations
- Rapid variability near black holes.
- Turbulent, time-dependent accretion flows.
- Strong magnetic fields near horizon scales.

### NRCB Requirements
- Accretion dynamics must remain unaffected externally.
- No persistent electromagnetic signatures of exotic geometry.

### Compliance Strategy
The null ring and chronology bubble do not act as luminous or reflective surfaces.  
All observed emissions originate from Regions B and C.

---

## Absence of Observed Causality Violations

### Observations
- No duplicated astrophysical events.
- No reversed-time signals detected.
- Global causal structure appears intact.

### NRCB Requirements
- Chronology violation must be localized and non-generic.
- No spontaneous causal loops accessible externally.

### Compliance Strategy
The chronology bubble is compact, highly constrained, and requires finely tuned trajectories.

## Conclusion

The NRCB model is viable only insofar as all exotic causal structure is confined to regions currently inaccessible to observation. Any detected deviation from Kerr-like behavior in accessible regimes would immediately falsify the model.
